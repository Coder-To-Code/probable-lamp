      • The Satyam scandal was a corporate fraud that took place at the India-based outsourcing company Satyam Computer Services. The scandal involved the company's founder and directors falsifying accounts, inflating the share price, and stealing money from the company. The money was largely invested in property.

      The scandal was discovered in late 2008 when the Hyderabad property market crashed, and the trail led back to Satyam. In 2009, the company's chairman, Byrraju Ramalinga Raju, confessed to the fraud and resigned from the board. Tech Mahindra purchased Satyam in April 2009 and renamed it Mahindra Satyam. Mahindra Satyam merged with Tech Mahindra on June 24, 2013.
